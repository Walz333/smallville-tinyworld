# Two-House-Garden v1 Scenario Contract

## Summary

`two-house-garden-v1` is a calm two-house-plus-garden horticultural microcosm. It is meant to express real plant work in scenario terms only, using small, reviewable work cycles rather than larger simulation machinery.

## Allowed World Facts

- Blue House, Green House, and Garden remain the only top-level spaces.
- Work may involve watering rounds, plant health review, tray and container checks, propagation or bed tidy work, sheltered-space inspection, tea pauses, and note review.
- Garden facts should stay concrete and local: beds, trays, containers, benches, gates, notebooks, tools, and clearly described plant-care states.
- Scenario details may support calm preparation for sale-ready stock review, but only as an observational readiness check, not as a trading system.

## Daily Rhythm

- Begin with tea or a short practical check-in before the first horticultural work block.
- Early work should favor water checks, sheltered-space inspection, tray/container checks, and note review.
- Midday work can move into watering, planting, propagation tidy, bed review, and calm stock-readiness checks.
- Tea and meal checkpoints act as pauses that reset the pace and keep the houses socially grounded.
- End-of-block work should close with tidy/status review and next-step notes rather than abrupt world expansion.

## Task Loop

The preferred loop for this scenario family is:

1. Water check
2. Barrel review
3. Greenhouse or sheltered-space inspection
4. Container / tray check
5. Plant health review
6. Planting or propagation / bed tidy
7. Path/gate check
8. Tea / meal checkpoint
9. Sale-ready stock review
10. Note-making / plan review
11. Tidy/status review

Not every tick needs every class, but activity should stay recognizably inside this horticultural loop.

## Out Of Scope Actions

- Live inventory systems
- Pricing logic
- Sensors or environmental devices
- SEPA feeds or outside data connections
- Commerce mechanics
- Persistence across restart
- Automatic proposal approvals
- Engine-facing contract changes
- Sudden world expansion beyond the two-house-plus-garden frame

## Success Metrics

- Actions remain readable as calm horticultural or domestic work.
- Watering, plant review, tray work, and sheltered-space checks appear as coherent scenario activity.
- Tea and pause checkpoints keep the rhythm grounded rather than interruptive.
- Sale-ready stock review remains observational and bounded.
- Notes and tidy/status reviews make the scenario easy to audit after a short run.

## Review Expectations

- Reviewers should be able to classify major actions against the approved task taxonomy.
- Location use should remain valid and easy to follow.
- Proposal behavior, if present, should stay bounded to calm domestic or horticultural additions.
- Run review should focus on clarity, coherence, and boundedness rather than on simulated scale.
